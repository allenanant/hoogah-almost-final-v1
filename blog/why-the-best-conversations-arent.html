Why the Best Conversations Aren’t Found in Crowded Rooms

If networking feels broken, maybe it’s the design, not the people. Let’s try something better.

Joe Lovett November 20, 2025

Last week, I wrote about why networking feels broken.
Since then, I’ve heard from so many founders who said the same thing:

“I go to these events and still leave without really meeting anyone.”

I get it. I was just at one of those packed meetups. It was a great turnout, proof (despite all that bad economic data coming out) that the entrepreneurial spirit is alive and well. But it was impossible to actually connect. The conversations were few and brief, the noise was high, and everyone seemed to know someone else already.

We’ve built entire industries around “networking,” yet the way most of us do it still feels off. Too rushed. Too surface. Too much effort for too little connection.

So in a few hours, (3pm ET ) we’re trying something different.

It’s called Networking Without the $19 Cocktail and it’s a one-hour experiment in slowing down and bringing intention back into how we meet people.
No rooms to work. No small talk to survive. Just a real one-on-one conversation with someone who actually wants to connect.

And even if connection isn’t your priority right now, we’d still love your feedback.
What’s working? What doesn’t? What should we build next?

This is a beta, and you can help shape something that could benefit a lot of people trying to build something meaningful.

If you’ve ever walked away from a networking event thinking, “I wish I’d met one person who really got it,” this is for you.
