Horses Run in Herds. Birds Fly in Flocks.

You can build alone. You just shouldn’t have to.

Khushi Yadav March 04, 2026

Photo by Vladimir Vujeva on Unsplash

In Vietnamese culture, there’s a saying:

Horses run in herds. Birds fly in flocks.
It does not mean a horse cannot run alone or a bird fly alone.
It means they were never meant to.

And yet, that is how many entrepreneurs build.

From quiet corners of coffee shops.
Making big decisions in a vacuum.
Carrying doubt privately.
Wondering if everyone else somehow has it figured out.

They don’t.

What many of them do have is something else.

Proximity.
Community.

You can run alone.
You just will not run as far.

Wealth Starts With Identity. Community Starts With Showing Up.

On March 8th in Cupertino, Liz Nguyen is hosting the Show Up & Link Up Women’s Entrepreneurial Summit.

It is intentionally designed for women building something who do not want to build it in isolation.

Inside the room:

  • A wealth strategy workshop with Denise Do challenging the idea that creatives are not good with money

  • Editorial portraits to elevate personal brand and identity

  • Small group masterminding

  • Curated 1:1 conversations designed to help women find their herd

And this last part is where most events fall short.

Community does not form in ballrooms.
It forms in conversations.

Not accidental ones.
Designed ones.

Hoogah is supporting the Link Up sessions to structure meaningful 1:1 connections so no one leaves saying, “I didn’t really meet anyone.”

Intentional connection is infrastructure.
Not a happy hour.

The Real Edge

Most people think advantage comes from strategy.

But the real edge is proximity to:

The right people. The right conversations. The right perspectives.

Horses run in herds. Birds fly in flocks.

Women building boldly should not have to do it alone.

If you are in the Bay Area and looking for your herd, this might be your room.
