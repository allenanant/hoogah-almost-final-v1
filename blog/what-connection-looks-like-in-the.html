What Connection Looks Like in the Wild

What happens when your big idea meets real people, real noise, and a real happy hour.

Joe Lovett & Khushi Yadav October 16, 2025

The sun was sinking over the Arts District in Los Angeles.
People were checking in, grabbing drinks, and catching up with old friends.
There was no stage, no slides, no microphone, just energy, laughter, and conversation.

And in the middle of it all, Hoogah was helping people connect.

We had the honor of powering the incredible ACE NextGen Unity Conference Welcome Reception, an evening built around entrepreneurship, culture, and community. Our goal was simple: create meaningful connections in a casual, open setting. Not a workshop, not a conference session, just a space for people to meet someone new, kick off a great event, and actually feel like they belonged.

We set up posters, QR codes, and table numbers. As people arrived, they downloaded the app, answered a few short questions, and were paired with someone who shared a similar background, goal or challenge. Within minutes, the space felt more intentional.

It wasn’t perfect. But it was real.

What Worked

People loved the idea of intentional networking.
Some told us it gave them the push they needed to talk to someone they wouldn’t have otherwise.
Others said it made the room feel warmer, more open, more human.

Even in an unstructured happy hour, you could feel the shift.
When conversations have purpose, people show up differently. They listen more. They stay longer. They leave feeling lighter.

What We Learned

Connection isn’t automatic. The real world has a way of revealing every friction point.

  • Some users pre-downloaded older versions of the app linked to past events.

  • Others hit authentication snags or struggled with data since the venue didn’t have Wi-Fi.

  • Without a PA system, prompting everyone to check their pairings proved tricky in a loud outdoor bar.

  • A few pairs missed each other entirely, while others found each other through quick thinking and persistence.

But that’s how it goes when you’re designing for human connection; human behavior likes to throw curve balls. The environment matters. The flow matters. People need simple cues and confidence to act on them.

We left knowing that connection design isn’t about perfect code, it’s about real conditions and real people.

How We’re Responding

Every challenge became feedback for what to improve next:

  • A smoother login experience

  • Profile pictures to make it easier to find your pair

  • Notifications when pairings are ready (for when there’s no mic)

  • Simpler prompts to help people feel guided, not lost

The app worked, and people connected. But we also learned that “effortless connection” requires constant refining and removing the little barriers that stand between curiosity and conversation.

The Bigger Lesson

If connection were easy, the world wouldn’t feel so lonely.

But even in a crowded bar with no program or announcements, people leaned in. They wanted to connect. And when it clicked, it really clicked.

That’s the heart of what we’re building. Not just software, but a bridge between strangers who might have more in common than they think.

We’re not chasing perfection. We’re learning how to design for belonging in the unpredictable, beautiful mess that is real life.

Closing Note

We’re still in beta, still learning, still building.
But every event like this brings us one step closer to making connection feel natural again.
