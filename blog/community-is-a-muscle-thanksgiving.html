Community Is a Muscle. Thanksgiving Reminds Us to Strengthen It

In a volatile world, the muscle we ignore is the one we end up needing most.

Joe Lovett & Khushi Yadav November 26, 2025

Thanksgiving week has a way of slowing everything down, for us to notice the people around us.

Actual people, with their lives, stories, and imperfections. The stuff connection is made of.

And it reminds us of something we forget almost all year:

Community is a muscle.
If you don’t use it, it weakens.
If you strengthen it, it gets more powerful.

We’re Living in a World That Makes Community Harder. And More Important

The modern workplace is faster, noisier, and more transactional than ever.

Teams are scattered.
Priorities shift constantly.
Attention is fragmented across apps, meetings, and inbox tabs.
People feel more isolated even while being constantly connected.

And yet, everything that truly moves us forward; ideas, support, resilience, momentum, comes from connection.

We forget this until a moment like Thanksgiving slows the pace just enough for us to feel it again.

Thanksgiving Is a Ritual. But Also a Reminder

Thanksgiving isn’t just a holiday, it’s a reconnection ritual.

You sit down.
You talk.
You ask actual questions.
You notice each other.

It’s intentionally human.

It’s a template for something we desperately need more of at work:
purposeful time where people show up as people, not roles or functions.

Because humans don’t bond on command.
They bond through moments.

Community Doesn’t Build Itself. It’s Designed

Inside organizations, we often assume connection “just happens.”

It doesn’t.

Like any muscle, it requires:

Rituals – regular touchpoints that create belonging
Structure – ways to connect that don’t feel forced
Meaning – conversations that go deeper than status updates
Consistency – not once a quarter, but ongoing

Events definitely help: SKOs, town halls, offsites, but even those need intention.

Leave connection to chance and you get cliques, surface-level banter, and people politely checking out.
Design connection and you get trust, collaboration, and a community that can weather uncertainty.

A Thought for Leaders (and Humans) This Week

What if we treated connection the way we treat fitness or creativity, something worth practicing?

What if:

• Teams had regular moments to talk like humans
• Leaders listened as often as they directed
• Organizations built rituals that kept people close, even when distributed
• Being known became as important as being productive

Strong organizations aren’t the ones with perfect processes.
They’re the ones where people feel connected enough to support each other through imperfect ones.

As You Head Into the Holiday…

Pay attention to how connection feels this week.

Warm.
Grounding.
Restoring.

Ask yourself:

What would your team or community look like if it felt like this more often?

That’s the possibility we’re building toward.
